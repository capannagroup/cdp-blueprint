# Traceability

## Overview

Traceability enables complete tracking of every manufactured product, component, material, and production activity throughout the manufacturing lifecycle.

Each manufactured item receives a unique identifier (Barcode, QR Code, or RFID) that links engineering, production, inventory, quality, and shipment records.

---

## Objectives

- Track every component
- Identify production history
- Improve quality investigations
- Support recalls if required
- Monitor production performance
- Ensure customer confidence

---

## Traceability Levels

### Material Level

- MDF Sheets
- WPC Boards
- Plywood
- Hardware
- Edge Banding
- Paint

---

### Component Level

Each manufactured component includes:

- Component ID
- Product
- Material Batch
- CNC Machine
- Operator
- Production Date

---

### Assembly Level

Each cabinet or door records:

- Product Number
- Components Used
- Assembly Operator
- Assembly Date
- Quality Inspection

---

### Finished Product

Every finished product stores:

- Serial Number
- Customer Order
- Production Order
- Shipment
- Warranty

---

## Barcode System

Each barcode contains:

- Production Order
- Work Order
- Product
- Component
- Machine
- Operator
- Date
- Shift

---

## QR Code Information

QR codes provide access to:

- Engineering Drawings
- BOM
- Routing
- CNC Programs
- Inspection Results
- Assembly Instructions

---

## Events Recorded

- Material Received
- Material Issued
- CNC Started
- CNC Finished
- Edge Banding
- Drilling
- Assembly
- Inspection
- Packaging
- Shipment
