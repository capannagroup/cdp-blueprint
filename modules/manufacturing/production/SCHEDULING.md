# Scheduling

## Overview

Scheduling converts production plans into executable manufacturing schedules by assigning work orders to machines, work centers, operators, and shifts while considering capacity, priorities, and material availability.

---

## Objectives

- Optimize production flow
- Reduce idle time
- Balance workloads
- Meet customer due dates
- Maximize machine utilization
- Minimize bottlenecks

---

## Scheduling Levels

### Master Schedule

- Weekly Planning
- Monthly Planning

### Detailed Schedule

- Daily Production
- Shift Planning
- Machine Assignment
- Operator Assignment

### Real-Time Scheduling

- Dynamic Rescheduling
- Priority Changes
- Machine Breakdowns
- Material Delays

---

## Scheduling Inputs

- Production Orders
- Work Orders
- Machines
- Work Centers
- Operators
- Material Availability
- Customer Priority
- Machine Capacity
- Shift Calendar

---

## Scheduling Rules

Examples include:

- Earliest Due Date
- FIFO (First In First Out)
- Highest Priority
- Shortest Processing Time
- Least Setup Time
- Critical Customer Orders

---

## Constraints

Scheduling considers:

- Machine Availability
- Tool Availability
- Labor Availability
- Material Availability
- Maintenance Windows
- Shift Calendars

---

## Outputs

Scheduling generates:

- Daily Schedule
- Machine Schedule
- Operator Schedule
- Shift Schedule
- Expected Completion Dates
