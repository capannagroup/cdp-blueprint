# Quality Control

## Overview

Quality Control ensures that manufactured products meet engineering specifications, customer requirements, and company quality standards throughout the production process.

Quality inspections are performed from incoming materials through finished products.

---

## Objectives

- Prevent manufacturing defects
- Standardize inspections
- Reduce rework
- Improve customer satisfaction
- Ensure product compliance
- Maintain quality records

---

## Inspection Stages

### Incoming Inspection

- Raw Materials
- Hardware
- Accessories
- Purchased Components

### In-Process Inspection

- CNC Operations
- Drilling
- Edge Banding
- Assembly
- Surface Finish

### Final Inspection

- Finished Product
- Packaging
- Label Verification
- Shipment Approval

---

## Inspection Types

- Visual Inspection
- Dimensional Inspection
- Functional Test
- Surface Finish Inspection
- Color Verification
- Hardware Verification

---

## Inspection Results

Each inspection records:

- Inspection ID
- Product
- Production Order
- Work Order
- Inspector
- Inspection Date
- Quantity Checked
- Quantity Passed
- Quantity Failed
- Defect Type
- Corrective Action
- Approval Status

---

## Defect Categories

Examples include:

- Dimension Error
- Edge Banding Defect
- Drilling Error
- Surface Damage
- Color Difference
- Assembly Error
- Missing Hardware
- Packaging Defect

---

## Corrective Actions

- Rework
- Repair
- Scrap
- Replace
- Engineering Review
- Process Improvement

---

## Quality KPIs

- First Pass Yield
- Defect Rate
- Rework Rate
- Scrap Rate
- Customer Returns
- Inspection Pass Rate
